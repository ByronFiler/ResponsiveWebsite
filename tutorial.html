Writing a Formal Report: A Guide

Page Summary

This page shall detail how one can produce an excellent quality formal report, based on a structure of five subsections:

  • Introduction
  • Discussion
  • Arguments and Counter-arguments
  • Conclusions
  • References & Sources

Introduction

The Introduction is the first stage of your report, it should entail what it is you intend to prove/disprove/present and how you plan to accomplish this. Ideally this should be relatively short and easy to read to give the reader a good idea of what they will be reading, hence try to avoid going into excessive detail and leave it to the other sections where what you'd like to talk about can be covered in as much detail as you feel is necessary.


Discussion

This section should act as a more complex expansion on the introduction where you should explain a more wider view and the arguments you will be comparing or the details you will be looking at.

  • Why are you looking at these things?
  • What are the wider implications of the report?
  • What are the benefits to this information?

This should contain all information pertaining to the report that would be useful for a reader to better understand the rest of the document as well as for what the document is for, it should ideally be a larger section than the introuction but shorter than the arguments and counter argumnets and main body section.


Arguments and Counter-arguments

Arguments and Counter-Arguments should be your largest section where you cover the bulk of what you want to discuss, it should contain all information relevant to discussion analysed and compared, if you're comparing two arguments it is recommended to do two subsections for and against something or containing the arguments for each side.


Conclusions

You should show your finding here based on what you've found from your arguments and which you feel is stronger, it should contain all relevant information to your conclusion. This should ideally be shorter than your full comparison of the two arguments as it should contain exclusively what is important from each.


References & Sources

This section should be relevatively short and contain all links and page references to information you've cited in your report in a simple and easily accesibly format, you should include full url links or for books, the author, book name, page and line number.
